Talk at Nanyang Girls' High School: Introduction to Japanese Culture and Society

Our department was invited by Nanyang Girls' High School (NYGH) to give a lecture introducing Japanese culture and society at their Sabbatical 2010 on 21 May.

The NYGH Sabbatical course was meant to give the students an understanding of Japanese culture by looking at both traditional and popular culture to examine their influence on all aspects of Japanese society such as business, politics, art, film and music.

 


The lecture by our department staff started off the NYGH Sabbatical course which involved lessons on basic Japanese language, hands-on sessions on Japanese traditional tea ceremony and dance, and interactions with cosplayers in Singapore.

 


Ms Yuen Shu Min gave an introduction about the Department of Japanese Studies and invited the students to think about the definition of Japanese culture in her section, “What is ‘Japanese Culture'?”

 


A summary of the history of foreign influences on Japanese art was given by Ms Carolyn Pang to highlight the complex developments of Japanese aesthetics in her section, “What? Wa -art (和 -art)?”

 


Ms Itsuko Tanaka described the developments of Japanese food, cross-cultural identities and localization in her talk, “Japanese Food- Culture and Identities.” She distributed matcha-chocolate biscuits to encourage the students to think about what “Japanese” taste is.

 


In his section, “Working to be a ‘Japanese',” Mr Kam Thiam Huat talked about how certain beliefs such as egalitarianism and self-responsibility lead to specific behavior in Japanese society.

 

Students were encouraged during the tasting and hands-on sessions to try samples of Japanese food and feel the kimono fabric swatches for a better understanding of the developments of Japanese food and art.
