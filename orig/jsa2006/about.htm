Japanese Studies Association in Southeast Asia

The Japanese Studies Association in Southeast Asia (JSA-ASEAN) was set up in 2005 to serve as a catalyst in promoting Japanese studies in this region. It is the only organization with the aim of bringing together major Japanese studies specialists from various Southeast Asian countries with their counterparts from Japan and other countries. It is hoped that with the combined resources of Japanese studies specialists in Southeast Asia, JSA-ASEAN would grow to become an important platform for the promotion of region-wide Japanese studies.
  

In order to stimulate interest in and encourage research and teaching on Japanese Studies in Southeast Asian countries and internationally by promoting scholarly networks based on Southeast Asia, JSA-ASEAN will organize a biennial conference as the major platform for a trans-disciplinary international exchange. The inaugural conference will be hosted by Department of Japanese Studies, National University of Singapore in October 2006, in conjunction with the 25th anniversary celebration of the Department of Japanese Studies and as an event marking the 40th anniversary of the diplomatic relations between Japan and Singapore. JSA-ASEAN also supports the organization of a national Japanese studies association within the Southeast Asian countries, and work towards close collaboration with other international association of Japanese studies. There are also plans to publish e-newsletters to help bridge geographical distance in the region; convene discipline-oriented sub-groups through workshops or lectures including the Japanese language and publications such as periodic journals.
