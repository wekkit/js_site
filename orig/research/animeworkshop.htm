Japanese Anime and Video Game Workshop with Pros

Organized by the Japanese Agency for Cultural Affairs and VIPO (Visual Industry Promotion Organization)
Supported by NUS Department of Japanese Studies and MDA (Media Development Authority)

National University of Singapore
Department of Japanese Studies

24 - 28 February 2014
10:00 - 16:30
Venue NUS FASS, Seminar Room (TBA)



Workshop Description:
This workshop consists of two seminars, Animation and Game, to introduce the latest trend of creative technologies in Japan which covers all visual content production. Over the past few decades, animation and games in Japan have become widespread, attracting legions of fans irrespective of age or gender, and developed its own “culture”. And now they have become both a culture and an industry that Japan is proud of. The latest artworks, manga, anime and games are highly regarded, and currently trends under the term “Cool Japan”, and has become a global phenomenon. While considering the question “Why Japanese animation and games are supported and evaluated as culture?” and their characteristics from the creative point of view, we will study the basics of animation and games and comprehensively analyze the contents of both categories. These two seminars will also be of help to research possible business opportunities or to research the relationship between modern Japanese culture and the world.

Learning Goals:
Production technologies are said to be difficult to learn and hard to practice if one does not have creativity, and there are only few who are born with such talents. However recent advances in digital technology have enabled everyone interested to make a better visual content. Digital technology has made it possible to analyze and categorize visual content production technologies as two parts: one is creative technologies and the other is the connective technologies.

For the Animation seminar, the students will examine the following:
1) Creative technologies at a glance, featuring Producing and Directing. 2) Script Writing, Script analysis and Script evaluation. 3) Dynamic character making, Character Evaluation and Introducing a DREAM method developed at the Creative Lab at TUT. 4) Mise-en-scene rendering, one of the latest concept of making a better quality visual content. 5) Problems of visual production in Japan, and then a final section for Questions and Answers.

For Game seminar, the students will take a step further from the state of simply saying "I like playing video games and I am interested in games.", and deepen their consideration for games and game creators. For game production, having imagination and an objective mindset will have a significant impact. The students will learn the elements necessary for game production such as design, character making, story-telling, visual styles, artwork design, etc. This will help the students to develop their own thinking and perspectives of games through workshops.

The goal of the workshop is to help the students understand the easiness of obtaining the technology and the joy of practicing the technologies when they write scripts, create characters and make animation and games. The technology and ideas of animation and games are constantly advancing, and possibilities abound for those who aspire to create the future.

In addition to lectures and seminars, workshop instruction will be administered through film screenings.

Students who attend both seminars of the workshop in full will receive a Certificate of Completion from VIPO.
