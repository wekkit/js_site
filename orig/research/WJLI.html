Workshop on Japanese Language and Interaction


National University of Singapore
Department of Japanese Studies

6-7 September 2013
Venue: AS4/03-28, JS Meeting Room

The Department of Japanese Studies at the National University of Singapore is honored to host the international Workshop on Japanese Language and Interaction.

Data Session
Lead by Shimako Iwasaki (Monash University)

Please click here for abstract. 


This workshop has been convened to explore Japanese language and interaction through the examination of video-recorded naturalistic data in order to investigate how Japanese speakers organize their social lives through the moment-to-moment co-construction of talk-in-interaction. Day 1 focuses on caregiver-child interactions, examining the ways that novices in a community are socialized in how to participate in interaction and how to understand (or to use) Japanese language as a resource to achieve pragmatic ends. Speakers of the talks are participants of the Cultural Formation of Responsibility in Caregiver-child Interactions (CCI) research project (Primary Investigator: Dr. Akira Takada, Associate Professor, Graduate School of Asian and African Area Studies, Kyoto University). This on-going research project has been investigating the methods by which ‘responsibility’ is formed culturally through interaction between children and care-givers. The project team has already recorded over 500 hours of Japanese children’s interaction with caregivers, which captures child’s development from its very early stages (0 to 5 years old). Anyone interested in Japanese language, interactional linguistics, language socialization, developmental pragmatics, and conversation analysis are welcome. All talks presented on Day 1 are in English.


Day 2 is a data session lead by Dr. Shimako Iwasaki (Monash University) examining video-recordings of naturally occurring ordinary and multi-party conversations amongst speakers of Japanese (both native and nonnative). The session will focus on multimodal embodied interaction. Data session workshops are the most common collaborative research methodology used by Conversation Analysis practitioners. Researchers from different disciplinary backgrounds get together in these sessions to examine and cross-analyze the specific stretch of conversational data that one of the researchers has selected from the larger data-set. These researchers together carefully examine all the recordings and transcripts of the selected data that owner of the data is attempting to analyze. The researchers collaboratively work to formulate a cross-disciplinary understanding of the phenomenon. All the participants can focus on any aspect of the interaction to the analysis, but that analysis has to be grounded in the data itself. Since participants will be an interdisciplinary group consisting with anthropologists, sociologists, and linguists, we all bring our own expertise and collaboratively analyze the data with our specific disciplinary knowledge. All discussion for this data session, like the data itself, will be done in Japanese. Anyone who understands Japanese and wants to participate in this collaborative learning opportunity is welcome.
