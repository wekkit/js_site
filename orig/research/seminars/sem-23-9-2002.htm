A TENTATIVE STUDY ON RESTRUCTURING OF EVERYDAY SOCIAL ORDERS: JAPAN, VIETNAM AND SINGAPORE

Event details
Speaker: Professor Hashimoto Kazutaka (Kanto Gakuin University)
Date: Monday, 23 September 2002
Time: 3.00 pm
Venue: AS4/03-28 (JS Staff Meeting Room)

Abstract

I am interested in the surface level of everyday life associated with moral behaviors. Changes of social orders at this level can bring about cultural changes, lead to a crisis of social identities and result in a cultural crisis. I will examine how some aspects of social order at the surface level of everyday life are being constructed, broken, and restructured. Such aspects include traffic behaviors, coloring of hair, using of mobile phones, graffiti, escalator rules, etc. This study is based on the observations and experiences of everyday life that I have had in Vietnam, Singapore and Japan. The aim of the study is not to suggest a convergence of societies but to show the realities of societies, from the viewpoint of a Japanese scholar.

About the speaker

Professor Hashimoto is a visiting scholar at the Department of Japanese Studies, NUS. He graduated from Hosei University and obtained his PhD from Nagoya University. He has been Professor at the Department of Applied Sociology, Kanto Gakuin University, since 1995. He was appointed Head of the Graduate School, College of Humanities, since 2000. Professor Hashimotos research interests are social planning, sociology of region and community, sociology of consumerism and social research history. He has published widely in his areas of interest, including 11 books (in Japanese) and various articles in both English and Japanese.
