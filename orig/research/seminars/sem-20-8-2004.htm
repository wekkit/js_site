AN EXPLORATORY STUDY OF COMPUTER-MEDIATED SOCIAL SUPPORT AMONG SINGLE MOTHERS IN JAPAN

Event details
Speaker: Ms Soong Lai Yee, Juliana (MA candidate, Department of Japanese Studies, NUS)
Date: 20 August 2004, Friday Time: 3:00pm 4:30pm
Venue: AS4/03-28 (JS Staff Meeting Room)

Abstract

Japan is experiencing a prolific increase in online self-help and social support groups and Japanese women are now becoming a vital part of its Internet community. Besides the social stigma associated with single motherhood, single mothers in Japan have to assume both caregiver and breadwinner roles within the family. Many have found their status a liability in developing social relationships and face estrangement from their families, reducing their much needed sources of social support. This study intends to investigate the types of social support being exchanged in an online bulletin board designated for single mothers in Japan . Optimal matching theory will be used as a framework to predict the types of support single mothers are seeking online. A preliminary content analysis of 100 postings reveals that the online bulletin board serves as an outlet for emotional expression and informational exchange among Japanese single mothers.

About the speaker

Soong Lai Yee, Juliana is a MA candidate at the Department of Japanese Studies, NUS. She received a Bachelor's degree majoring in Japanese Studies and Sociology in 2002 and completed her Honours degree in 2003, graduating with 2nd Upper Honours in Japanese Studies.
