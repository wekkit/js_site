TOKYO IN GLOBAL ECONOMY: ECONOMIC RESTRUCTURING, URBAN POLICY AND STRATEGIC PLANNING

Event details
Speaker: Mr Asato Saito (PhD candidate, Department of Geography and Environment, London School of Economics and Political Science)
Date: Wednesday, 6 November 2002
Time: 3.00 4.30 pm
Venue: AS4/03-28 (JS Staff Meeting Room)

Abstract

In this seminar, the speaker will present how Tokyo's economic structure, and the direction of its urban policy and strategic planning, has evolved over the last twenty years by reviewing various social and economic indicators, and policy documents. It shows a pattern of policies corresponding to the economic situation. The optimistic and forward looking tone in urban policy in the 1980s when Tokyo enjoyed economic boom has given way to a more aggressive and entrepreneurial tone which put more emphasis on competitiveness since the late 1990s in the face of prolonged recession and uncertainty. The speaker will also argue that this development may have implications on the way policy is made. The traditional state-centered economic development model has attracted criticisms from those who advocate less governmental intervention.

About the speaker

Mr Asato Saito was born and educated in Japan up to the undergraduate level. He has conducted research in Britain over the last ten years leading to his PhD. His main research interest is to explore the interaction between globalisation and economic/social/political change in the worlds leading metropolises. His ongoing projects include analyzing economic restructuring and urban policy through the strategic planning and urban development projects of London and Tokyo.
