The Silver Market Phenomenon in Japan Implications of Demographic Change on Business and Marketing in the World's Most Aged Society

Event details
Speaker : Dr Florian Kohlbacher
               German Institute for Japanese Studies
Date      : Tuesday, 3 May 2011
Time      : 2:00pm - 4:00pm
Venue    : AS7/01-01

Abstract

Demographic change has emerged as a powerful trend affecting a large number of countries around the world. The ageing, and in some cases shrinking of the population has vast overall individual, social, economic and organisational consequences.  One essential implication of this demographic shift is the emergence and constant growth of the so-called “greying market” or “silver market”, that is, the market segment more or less broadly defined as those people aged 50 and older.  Many companies lack the necessary experience, processes and know-how to develop suitable products for the silver market and to market them efficiently and successfully. Moreover, an in-depth understanding of the consumer behaviour of older people is also missing.

This presentation aims to give an introduction to the silver market phenomenon in Japan and the entailing challenges and opportunities.  Based on empirical research in Japan, findings pertaining to an understanding of consumer behaviour and the attitudes of older Japanese as well as the responses by business enterprises in terms of new product development, marketing strategies, advertising, as well as corporate social responsibility will be presented and discussed.

About the speaker

Dr Florian Kohlbacher is a Senior Research Fellow and currently Deputy Director at the German Institute for Japanese Studies (DIJ) Tokyo.  He heads a research project on the business implications of demographic change in Japan. Florian holds both a master's degree and a doctorate from the Vienna University of Economics and Business, Austria.  Prior to joining the DIJ in 2007, he has been a Japanese Government (MEXT) Scholarship Visiting Researcher at Hitotsubashi University in Tokyo for two years. Florian is a Fellow of the World Demographic & Ageing Forum as well as an Advisor to the International Mature Marketing Network (IMMN).  He is the author of “International Marketing in the Network Economy: A Knowledge-Based Approach”, Palgrave, 2007, and co-editor of “The Silver Market Phenomenon: Marketing and Innovation in the Aging Society”, 2nd edition, Springer, 2011.
