DISABILITY IN JAPAN

Event details
Speaker : Assoc Prof Carolyn Stevens
               University of Melbourne
Date      : Monday, 27 September 2010
Time      : 12:00noon - 3:00pm
Venue    : AS7 Auditorium (AS7/01-02)

Abstract

Stevens interrogates notions of disability in Japan throughout history, noting a shift from disability from the religious to the industrial sphere through modernity. Legal structures that support people with disability are also examined to help understand how Japanese society is called upon to deliver welfare programs. The concept of barrier free society is then problematized through a discussion of the relationship between an individual with a disability and his/her integration in wider Japanese society.

About the speaker

Carolyn Stevens is Associate Professor at the Asia Institute, the University of Melbourne. She received a PhD in anthropology from Columbia University. She has worked on contemporary social and cultural issues in Japan such as popular music and disability. Her main publications include Japanese Popular Music: Culture, Power and Authenticity (Routledge 2008) and On the Margins of Japanese Society: Volunteers and the Urban Underclass (Routledge 1997).
