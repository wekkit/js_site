TOWARD GLOCAL POPULAR VISUAL CULTURE STUDIES

Event details
Speaker : Dr Yuko Kikuchi
               Reader, CCW Graduate School and TrAIN Research Centre at University of the Arts London German
Date      : Monday, 8 August 2011
Time      : 2:00pm - 3:30pm
Venue    : AS4/03-28 (JS Meeting Room)

Abstract

‘Japanese Popular Culture' is an attractive field of study for students, but the way in which it has been framed has not been without problems.  Experts in this field question whether we can talk about the particularity of ‘Japan' given that ‘popular culture' is regarded as inherently global.  In my presentation I would like to suggest a way of approaching this study which enables the ‘glocal' perspective.  My presentation will initially outline the debate and draw upon my research, which investigates the development of discourses of ‘Japaneseness' in a global cultural environment.  It will also elaborate visual culture studies on which I have been focusing as a design historian and describe how a design historical approach is effective in dealing with this subject.  This will be followed by a detailed look at my current research about the US design intervention in Asia (Japan, Taiwan, Hong Kong, Vietnam, Cambodia and Thailand) with particular focus on the designer Russel Wright's projects. This research forms a highly fruitful case study for Glocal Popular Visual Culture Studies.  As Japan's case was taken as a model for a design development programme that was reused by the US in other parts of Asia, the talk will focus on Wright's project in Japan.  The key issue of this research is the way in which postwar national design developments in Japan are entwined with those of the US, in particular in the construction of ‘Japanese Modern' and ‘American Modern' design discourses within popular culture.  I will also discuss how ‘Japan' was widely consumed through household design products and Hollywood films.  The talk will finish with a summary of my vision for this field of study and what I would like to offer for Japanese Popular Culture courses.

About the speaker

Dr Yuko Kikuchi is Reader in the History of Art and Design at TrAIN (Research Centre for Transnational Art Identity and Nation), University of the Arts London.  She is also an editorial member of Journal of Design History. Her works include Mingei Theory and Japanese Modernisation: Cultural Nationalism and ‘Oriental Orientalism' (London: RoutlegeCurzon, 2004) and Refracted Modernity: Visual Culture and Identity in Colonial Taiwan (Honolulu: University of Hawai‘i Press, 2007).  She has also co-authored with Toshio Watanabe Ruskin the Japan 1890-1940: Nature for Art, Art for Life (Tokyo: Cogito, 1997).  She is working on a book about Russel Wright and the US intervention in Asian design during the Cold War, and currently at ARI in NUS as visiting senior research fellow, researching ‘Russel Wright and Southeast Asia' with particular focus on Cambodia and Thailand.  She is also leading a joint international project ‘Oriental Modernity: Modern Design Development in East Asia, 1920-1990' together with design historians in Japan, Korea, Hong Kong, Taiwan, Canada and the UK to promote studies on transnational design histories within this region.
