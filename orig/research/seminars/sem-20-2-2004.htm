A STUDY ON THE ERROR OF ADDITIONAL /NO/ BY SINGAPORE SECONDARY SCHOOL STUDENTS

Event details
Speaker: Mr Tan Chyn Ngian (MA candidate, Department of Japanese Studies, NUS)
Date: 20 February 2004, Friday
Time: 10:00am 11:30am
Venue: AS4/03-28 (JS Staff Meeting Room)

Abstract

This paper explores the possible causes of the error of additional particle /no/ found in the Japanese compositions of Singaporean secondary school year four students who have gone through four years of structured and continuous learning. There are earlier studies on this particular error but the participants in these studies were mainly university students learning Japanese or foreign students who were attempting to enter a university in Japan. Also, they tend to be either group of learners who share a common first language or those from different countries and do not share a common mother tongue. With regard to the study of the target errors, the 211 Singapore secondary school students constitute a group of younger subjects that have not been studied before. Also, the bilingual education environment in Singapore is an interesting factor with regard to the influence of the learners first language on second language acquisition.

Surveys, case study and error analysis were used in this study to find out if the target errors will diminish as the students advance in their learning and to explore the probable causes. It was found that students had difficulty identifying and correcting the target errors up to year four of secondary school. Also, through the case studies and error analysis, although Chinese and English interference were probable causes, overgeneralization of the usage of particles and fossilization of errors were also major factors.

About the speaker

Tan Chyn Ngian is a MA candidate at the Department of Japanese Studies, NUS. On a 5-year Monbusho scholarship, he studied and received his MA (Language and Area Studies) from the Tokyo University of Foreign Studies.
