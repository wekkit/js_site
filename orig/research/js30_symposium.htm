Japanese Studies 30th Anniversary Symposium:
"Sketching Japan: Diversity, Dynamics and Dilemmas"

 

Date:     28 October 2011
Time:    4:00pm-6:30pm
Venue:  Auditorium (2nd Storey)
              Shaw Foundation Alumni House
              National University of Singapore
              11 Kent Ridge Drive
              Singapore 119244


Chair: Assoc Prof Thang Leng Leng

The Department of Japanese Studies offers its students a comprehensive education on Japan and this is based on the wide scope of research interests pursued by its faculty members. The four presentations in the 30 th Anniversary Japanese Studies Symposium showcase the diverse research strength of the department. In particular, speakers will seek to dispel common notions that tend to paint an image of Japan as a monolithic, homogenous society. With our sketches of Japan ranging from pre-modern to post-modern times, we will be presenting Japan and Japanese Studies as an arena of dynamism, heterogeneity and contestation.

Assoc Prof Simon Avenell will begin with a discussion of the issues concerning volunteering and Japanese civil society in the aftermath of the recent triple disasters of Japan. Next, Dr Timothy Amos will present his findings on outcaste groups in Tokugawa Japan and illustrate the kinds of lives they tried to build for themselves. This will be followed by Assoc Prof Lim Beng Choo's presentation on elements of cultural borrowing manifested in Noh plays from the Muromachi period. Finally, Assoc Prof Hendrik Meyer-Ohle will present findings about consumption practices through the case study of shopping malls and streets in the context of Japan's ageing, shrinking and diversifying markets.
