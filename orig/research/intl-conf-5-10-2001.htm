INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE FOR THE 20TH ANNIVERSARY OF THE DEPARTMENT
MEMORY-MAKING IN JAPAN

Event details
5 - 6 October 2001
Shaw Foundation Building (AS7), Seminar Rooms A, B & C
Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences
National University of Singapore

Sponsored by the Japan Foundation

The origins of the Department of Japanese Studies, National University of Singapore can be traced back to 1979 when Mr Lee Kuan Yew, Prime Minister of Singapore, proposed the idea of establishing a department of Japanese Studies to his Japanese counterpart, Prime Minister Ohira Masayoshi. This idea was brought to realization when the department was officially set up in 1981, with assistance from the Japanese government. In commemoration of the 20th anniversary of the founding of the Department, an international conference on Japanese Studies, entitled "Asian Values and Japan's Options", will be held on October 56, 2001 at the Shaw Foundation Building (AS 7) at the National University of Singapore. The conference, financially supported by the Japan Foundation, aims to make a contribution to narrowing the gaps in our understanding of Asian values by examining wider the implications of the Asian value debate from four perspectives:

culture and history, with attention to national identity and Asian values;
international relations, especially in the context of regionalism;
politics on democracy and human rights; and
economy and business, with a special focus on state development and corporate management.

A total of 16 papers will be presented by leading academic experts from Japan, the United States, the United Kingdom, Australia and Southeast Asia.

We would consider it a great pleasure and honour if you would join us in celebrating the 20th anniversary of the Department by attending part or all of the symposium. We very much look forward to your participation.

Conference convener
Takashi Terada
