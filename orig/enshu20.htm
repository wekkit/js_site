Japanese Tea Ceremony with the NUS President (25 August 2010)


Professor Tan Chor Chuan, the President of NUS was invited to a Japanese tea ceremony performed by the 13th Grand Master of the Enshu Sado School, Grand Master Kobori Sojitsu.  

To commemorate the 20th anniversary of the Enshu Sado School at NUS, a lecture by Grand Master Kobori, “Sado as a Comprehensive Art Form - Kirei Sabi of Enshu Style” was held in the auditorium of the NUS Shaw Foundation Alumni House on 27 August 2010.

 

Grand Master Kobori performed the Japanese tea ceremony on a table which was specially designed by him for sado. The sado table, Tenrai ( 天籟 ) was presented as one of his gifts to the NUS Department of Japanese Studies.

 

Grand Master Kobori showed the various instruments of the Japanese tea ceremony and explained Japanese aesthetics to Professor Tan.

 

In appreciation of the continuous support and generous donations by the Enshu Sado School, Professor Tan presented Grand Master Kobori with a gift of his own art works, including a Chinese painting of China's Huangshan.

 

From left: Teachers of the Enshu Sado School, Dr Emi Morita, Mrs Kobori, Grand Master Kobori, Professor Tan, Associate Professors Hendrik Meyer-Ohle and Thang Leng Leng.
